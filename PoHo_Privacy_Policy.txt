How PoHo collects, uses, and protects your data

Version 1.0  |  Effective Date: 1 January 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how PoHo collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects your

personal data when you use our Services. Please read it carefully. Your use of the Services

constitutes your acceptance of this policy. PoHo is committed to compliance with the UK

General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.



1.  Introduction and Who We Are

PoHo Mobile ("PoHo", "we", "us", "our") is the data controller responsible for the personal data processed under this Privacy Policy. PoHo is a social discovery platform that helps people find events, activities, and companions for real-world experiences. Our Services include a mobile application (the "App"), a website at poho.world, and related features including real-time messaging, audio/video calling, location-based discovery, and event management.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data with transparency, integrity, and care. This Privacy Policy is written in plain English and is intended to be clear and understandable. Questions? Contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@poho.world.

1.1  Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data we collect when you:

Download, install, or register for the App;

Browse or use any feature of the Services;

Communicate with other users through the platform;

Contact us directly, including through our support channels.



This Policy does not apply to the practices of third-party services linked to or integrated with our Services. We strongly encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you use.

1.2  Legal Framework

PoHo processes personal data in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR), and all other applicable UK data protection legislation. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we take appropriate safeguards as described in Section 10 of this Policy.

2.  Information We Collect

We collect several categories of personal data depending on how you use the Services. We collect only the data that is necessary for the purposes described in this Policy.

2.1  Information You Provide Directly

Registration and Profile Data

When you create an account, we collect: your name or display name; email address; date of birth; gender (optional); profile photograph; and a password (stored using Argon2 cryptographic hashing — we never store plaintext passwords). If you register via Apple, Google, or Meta/Facebook SSO, we receive your name, email address, and profile picture from that provider.

Content You Create

We collect all content you create and share on the platform: posts, comments, reactions, photographs, videos, audio messages, event listings, and any other content you upload or transmit through the Services.

Messages

We process the content of messages you send to other users through the platform's real-time messaging features. Messages are transmitted via our WebSocket infrastructure and may be retained in our database until deleted by the user.

Event and Activity Data

When you create or join an event, we collect the event details you provide, your RSVPs and attendance confirmations, and any content you add to the event.

Support and Communications

If you contact us for support or submit a complaint, we collect your name, contact details, and a description of the issue.

2.2  Information Collected Automatically

Device and Technical Data

When you use the App, we automatically collect technical information including device model and identifiers, operating system version, app version and crash logs, network type and IP address, and FCM/APNs push notification tokens used to deliver notifications and route incoming calls to your device.

Usage Data

We collect information about how you interact with the Services — features accessed, time spent, actions taken, events viewed or joined, and other engagement metrics — used in aggregated and anonymised form to improve the Services.

Location Data

If you grant location permission, we collect real-time GPS data to power the "nearby events" feature, the "trips" feature, and other location-based functionality. We strongly recommend the "While Using the App" option. You can withdraw location permission at any time through your device settings.

Camera and Microphone Data

If you grant camera and microphone permissions, we access these only when you are actively using features that require them. We do not access your camera or microphone in the background.

2.3  Information From Third Parties

If you register via Apple Sign-In, Google Sign-In, or Meta (Facebook) Login, we receive account information from those providers. We also use Firebase and Google Cloud services for crash analytics, performance monitoring, and app usage telemetry.

4.  How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal data to any third party, ever.



We share your data only as described in this section and only where necessary to operate the Services or comply with legal obligations.

4.1  Other Users

Information you choose to make public — including your display name, profile photograph, bio, and event activity — will be visible to other users. You control the visibility of your profile information through your privacy settings.

4.2  Technology and Infrastructure Partners

We work with the following third-party technology providers who process personal data on our behalf, subject to binding data processing agreements:

Agora (Agora.io) — real-time audio and video call routing

Supabase — primary database (PostgreSQL) and object storage

Google LLC / Firebase — Android push notifications, Google Sign-In, crash reporting

Apple Inc. — iOS push notifications (APNs) and Apple Sign-In

Meta Platforms, Inc. — Facebook Login authentication

Redis — real-time WebSocket messaging infrastructure

Resend — transactional email delivery

Expo (EAS) — cloud builds, over-the-air updates, and push notification infrastructure



4.3  Legal Compliance and Safety

We may disclose your personal data to law enforcement or regulatory authorities where required by applicable law, court order, or government request, or to protect the safety of users or third parties.

4.4  Business Transactions

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or restructuring, your personal data may be transferred to a successor entity. We will notify you in advance and provide information about the new data controller's privacy practices.

5.  Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data. Key security measures include:

Argon2 password hashing — resistant to brute-force and GPU-based attacks; we never store passwords in plaintext

TLS 1.2+ encryption for all data in transit between the App and our servers

JWT authentication with short expiry windows and token rotation

Role-based database access controls and IP whitelisting

Encrypted Agora RTP protocols for audio and video calls



Access to personal data is restricted to personnel on a need-to-know basis. We will notify you and the ICO of any personal data breach within 72 hours where required.

7.  Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights. To exercise any of them, contact us at privacy@poho.world. We will respond within one calendar month.

7.1  Right of Access

You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a Subject Access Request). We will provide this information free of charge within one month.

7.2  Right to Rectification

You may correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data. Most profile information can be updated directly within the App.

7.3  Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or it has been unlawfully processed. You can delete your account through the App settings.

7.4  Right to Restriction of Processing

You may request that we restrict processing in certain circumstances, such as while a dispute about accuracy is being resolved.

7.5  Right to Data Portability

Where processing is by automated means on the basis of consent or contract, you may receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format and request transfer to another controller.

7.6  Right to Object

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will stop unless we can demonstrate compelling grounds that override your interests.

7.7  Right to Withdraw Consent

You may withdraw consent for location data, camera/microphone access, and push notifications at any time through your device settings.

7.8  Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe we have not handled your data lawfully, you may lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

8.  Cookies and Tracking

The PoHo mobile application does not use traditional browser cookies. We use Firebase Analytics within the App — which uses a randomly generated instance identifier, not a persistent device ID — to collect anonymised usage data. You may opt out through the App's privacy settings.

Our website at poho.world uses strictly necessary cookies and, with your consent, analytics cookies via Google Analytics. A cookie consent banner is presented on your first visit.

9.  Children's Privacy

The Services are not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. Features related to dating and meeting new people are restricted to users aged 18 or over. If you believe your child under 13 has provided us with personal data, please contact privacy@poho.world immediately.

10.  International Data Transfers

When we transfer your personal data outside the UK or EEA, we rely on UK Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, or other approved transfer mechanisms. Key international processors include Agora (USA), Google LLC (USA), Apple Inc. (USA), Meta Platforms (USA), and Supabase (USA). Contact privacy@poho.world for further information about safeguards.

11.  Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our Services or applicable law. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date, notify you via in-app notification or email, and where required by UK GDPR, seek fresh consent.
